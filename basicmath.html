Basic math in programming

In this section, we will learn how to do basic math in a programming language.

Operators

Yes, Python can do math as well! You can add numbers, subtract numbers, and more! Here's a list of things you can type in the window and press Enter/Return:

  • 3+4
  • 5-4
  • 8*5
  • 16/4
See? It gives you the solution to the math you typed in! There are more operators you can use, such as the modulus operator (%), which is the percent sign. The modulus operator when used, gives the remainder of a division problem, like 6%5=1. Type in 6%5 and press Enter/Return. It gives you the frigging remainder! You can also do exponents, by using two asterisks (**) between the numbers. Type in 10**2, then press Enter/Return. It gives you 100. Wow!

Order of operations

The order of operations also applies to math in programming. Remember PEMDAS (Please Enjoy My Delicious Alcoholic Sake)? The parentheses are first. The exponents are second. Third and fourth are multiplication and division in left to right order. Fifth and sixth are addition and subtraction, also in left to right order. Try this equation: (5+6)*3. Evaluate it on your own first and then type it in the Python window to see if you got the right answer. OK, how did you do? The answer was 33, not 23. You do the math in the parentheses first!

What the hell is a float?!/Types of numbers

There are different types of numbers in Python, such as integers and floats. An integer is the same as an integer in math. It is a number with no decimal point, like 5, 10, and 45. A float is kind of the opposite. It does have a decimal point, like 5.6, 7.34, and 34.76. There are more types of numbers, like doubles, longs, and a few others, but those are kind of irrelevant to Python. Those are more common in other programming languages like Java and C++.

Conclusion

You learned about math in programming and the types of numbers in programming!
